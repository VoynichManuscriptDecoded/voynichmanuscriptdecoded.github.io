29th October 2025

Research into Cosimo de’ Medici’s private gardens and early experiments made me look again at the plant on Folio 1v. I had identified it as lemon, but the evidence now points to bitter orange (Citrus aurantium). The morphology, especially the three-lobed cup and green-yellow leaves, fits orange far better, and historically this was the citrus Cosimo actually cultivated in Florence. Its sap is aromatic and tonic rather than acidic, which matches the page’s theme of male renewal and cleansing. This correction doesn’t change the decode – it strengthens it, tying the work even more closely to Medici solar medicine and Cosimo’s own fertility experiments.
